B2 Internship (2025) · Mission #1

Primary Achievement

Monitoring & Mapping — Making Info Actionable

VIApass Palais des Festivals (Cannes) Network Operations

Context

The need: **standardize mapping** and make **supervision** dashboards truly **actionable** (relevant indicators, contextualized thresholds, clear prioritization). Objective: save diagnosis time during peak load periods.

Operational Objectives

  • Unified **diagram templates** (legends, naming conventions, non-sensitive "public" views).
  • **"Event" View**: readable synthesis + drill-down by priority zone.
  • **Alerting** refocused on what is *actionable* (reduced noise, clear criticality).
  • Simple **diagnosis → escalation → post-mortem** playbooks to follow.

Approach

  1. **Audit** of existing diagrams/indicators (duplicates, divergences, unsuitable thresholds).
  2. **Standardization**: legends, naming, filters, and view templates.
  3. **Prototype** of an "Event" view (synthesis + links to details).
  4. **Tests** on small events → feedback collection → adjustments.
  5. **Documentation**: dissemination of templates, playbooks, pre-opening checklist.

Results & Impact

  • **Faster** diagnosis in sensitive zones (clear visual entry points).
  • More **relevant** alerts (reduced noise, better prioritization).
  • Improved Field ↔ NOC **coordination** (same "language," unitary actions).
Confidentiality: on published visuals, mask SSIDs/passwords, public IPs, hostnames, and sensitive port numbers.

Stack & Tools (Generic)

  • Monitoring: availability, load, RSSI, SNR, median throughput, alerting.
  • Incident Management: ticketing → diagnosis → escalation → synthetic post-mortem.
  • Docs: diagram templates ("public" export), playbooks, checklists.
  • Field: distribution case, switches (e.g., MikroTik), high-density APs, RJ45 testers.

Use Case (Anonymized)

Symptom: unstable throughput in the press zone during peak hours.

  • "Event" View → load peak + low SNR on Row B.
  • Action: distribution reinforcement + rebalancing, QoS check.
  • Result: stability restored, user comfort re-established.

Best Practices

  • **Uniform** maps + consistent naming → less ambiguity.
  • Alerts sorted by **criticality** + contextualized thresholds → actionable focus.
  • Ticketing with *who/what/where/when* → accelerated diagnosis.
  • Pre-opening brief & short debrief → continuous improvement.
